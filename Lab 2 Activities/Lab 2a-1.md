### Calculating Cost

### 5 Years calculation:

Hours running: 5 years * 52 weeks * 40 hours = 10400 hours

Pages used: 5 years * 52 weeks * 750 pages = 195000 pages

Cost of paper: 195000 pages / 500 pages * $7.90 = $3081

#### Inkjet:

Electricity used for 5 years: 10400 hours * 0.009kWh = 93.6 kWh

Price to run inkjet: 93.6 kWh * $0.3478 = $32.56

Ink cartridges to be used: 195000 pages / 300 pages = 650

Number of black/coloured for inkjet: 650/2 = 325 pages

Price of black cartridges for inkjet: 325 pages * $33 = $10725 / 5 years

Price of coloured cartridges for inkjet: 325 pages * $48 = $15600 / 5 years

Total price of cartridges: $10725 + $15600 = $26325

#### Total cost: $26325 + $79 + $32.56 + $3081 = $29517.56


#### Laser Printer

Electricity used for 5 years: 10400 hours * 0.07kWh = 728kWh

Price to run printer: 728 kWh * $0.3478 = $253.20

Black ink cartridges to be used: (195000 pages / 2) / 3000 = 32.5 -> 33

Coloured ink cartridges to be used: (19500 pages / 2) / 2300 = 42.39 -> 43

Price of black ink cartridges: 33 * $117 = $3861

Price of coloured ink cartridges: 43 * $138 = $5934

Total price of cartridges: $3861 + $5934 = $9795

#### Total cost: $9795 + $658 + $253.20 + $3081 = $13787.2

# REFLECTION QUESTIONS

- Based on the TCO which printer is the most cost effective over 5 years?

  **The Brother laser printer would be the better option because based on the TCO, it costs $13787.2 while inkjet printer costs $29517.56**

- Would your answer be the same for a home user who prints 5 pages per week?

  **No, because if the person only prints 5 pages per week, the need for ink cartridges would be way lower. Most of the cost came from the amount of ink cartridges used.**
  
- What other factors could/should we have considered apart from the TCO?

  **Print quality - Different printer would definitely produce different quality of paper outcome.
    Duplex printing - Automatic double-sided printing could contribute to convenience.
    Warranty - Different companies offer different package of warranty.
    Reliability - Usually laser printer is made for heavy usage in office setting, while inkjet is usually used for home light usage.**
  
- Knowing something about the cost structure of printers, what would you look for in a large workgroup printer?
  
  **I would look for a printer with high-capacity toner cartridges, fast print speeds, automatic duplex printing, and low cost per page.**
  
- What is the time period where both printers will cost the same?
  
**The two printers would cost approximately the same after 9 weeks (around 2 months) of use, based on the assumption of printing 750 pages per week. Although the laser printer has a much higher initial purchase price, its lower toner cost per page allows it to recover the extra cost quickly. After this break-even point, the laser printer becomes the more economical option and continues to save money over the remainder of the five-year period.**
